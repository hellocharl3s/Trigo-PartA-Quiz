Trig Ratios

🏗️ Engineers use these every day — to calculate the height of a building from a distance, or the angle a ramp must be built at. You're learning the same maths.
The 3 ratios — SOH CAH TOA
sin θ = opposite / hypotenuse cos θ = adjacent / hypotenuse tan θ = opposite / adjacent
PRO TIP
Finding a side? Put the unknown on top and multiply.
Finding an angle? Flip it → use sin⁻¹, cos⁻¹, or tan⁻¹.
⚠️ Opposite and adjacent swap depending on which angle you're using. Always identify your angle first before labelling sides.
🌗
Topic 2 of 5

Obtuse Angles

🎯 Your calculator only gives you the acute angle. For obtuse triangles, you need to know when to add a second solution — this trips up almost everyone.
The two rules
sin A = sin(180° − A) ← same value cos A = −cos(180° − A) ← flips sign
PRO TIP
sin x = k: TWO answers → sin⁻¹(k) and 180° − sin⁻¹(k)
cos x = negative: ONE answer → cos⁻¹ gives it directly
⚠️ For cosine, a negative result means obtuse. Don't subtract from 180° — your calculator already gives the right answer.

Topic 3 of 5

Area of a Triangle

🌍 Surveyors calculating land area, architects working out roof panels — whenever you have two sides and the angle between them, this formula is the one.
The formula
Area = ½ × b × c × sin A A is the included angle between sides b and c
PRO TIP
Parallelogram? Double it → Area = ab sin C
Find the angle? Rearrange → sin C = (2 × Area) / (ab)
⚠️ The angle must be between the two sides you're using. If it's not the included angle, the formula doesn't work directly.

Topic 4 of 5

Sine Rule

⚓ Two ships and a lighthouse — if you know the angles from each ship to the lighthouse and the distance between the ships, you can find any missing measurement. That's the sine rule.
The rule — use a matching pair
a/sin A = b/sin B = c/sin C Finding a side → put unknown on top Finding an angle → put sin? on top
PRO TIP
Ambiguous case: 2 sides + non-included angle, and the side opposite the given angle is shorter than the other → could be 2 triangles. Always check both!
⚠️ You must have a matched pair (a side and its opposite angle both known) to use this rule at all.
📡
Topic 5 of 5

Cosine Rule

🚀 GPS satellites, radar tracking, even game engines calculating collision angles — the cosine rule is the go-to when you have two sides and the angle clamped between them.
Finding a side (SAS) · Finding an angle (SSS)
a² = b² + c² − 2bc cos A Rearrange to find angle: cos A = (b² + c² − a²) / 2bc
PRO TIP
If cos A comes out negative, angle A is obtuse. The formula handles it automatically — no extra step.
Largest angle is always opposite the longest side.
⚠️ The angle you substitute must be opposite the side you're solving for. Mix these up and you'll get a wrong answer every time.
